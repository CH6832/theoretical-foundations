### 1. Newton’s Laws of Motion

1. **First Law Application:**
   - A book is placed on a table. Explain why it stays at rest, using Newton’s First Law.

2. **Second Law Calculation:**
   - A car of mass 800 kg accelerates at \(3 \, \text{m/s}^2\). Calculate the net force acting on the car.

3. **Third Law Interaction:**
   - If a person exerts a force of 100 N on a wall, what is the reaction force exerted by the wall on the person?

---

### 2. Forces and Dynamics

4. **Gravitational Force Calculation:**
   - Determine the gravitational force between two masses of \(10 \, \text{kg}\) and \(15 \, \text{kg}\) separated by \(2 \, \text{m}\). (Use \(G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N} \cdot \text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2\))

5. **Friction Force:**
   - A box with a mass of 50 kg is on a horizontal surface with a coefficient of friction \( \mu = 0.4\). Calculate the frictional force acting on the box.

6. **Tension in a Rope:**
   - A rope is pulling a sled with a force of 200 N at an angle of \(30^\circ\) above the horizontal. Determine the horizontal and vertical components of the tension.

---

### 3. Kinematics

7. **Displacement Calculation:**
   - An object moves from \(x_i = 5 \, \text{m}\) to \(x_f = 20 \, \text{m}\) in \(10 \, \text{s}\). Calculate the displacement.

8. **Velocity and Acceleration:**
   - An object’s velocity changes from \(5 \, \text{m/s}\) to \(15 \, \text{m/s}\) in \(4 \, \text{s}\). Determine its acceleration.

9. **Kinematic Equation Problem:**
   - A car accelerates uniformly from \(20 \, \text{m/s}\) to \(30 \, \text{m/s}\) over a distance of \(100 \, \text{m}\). Find the acceleration and the time taken.

---

### 4. Work and Energy

10. **Work-Energy Theorem:**
    - Calculate the work done by a force of \(500 \, \text{N}\) that moves an object \(10 \, \text{m}\) in the direction of the force.

11. **Kinetic Energy Change:**
    - Find the change in kinetic energy of an object with mass \(2 \, \text{kg}\) as its velocity increases from \(3 \, \text{m/s}\) to \(7 \, \text{m/s}\).

12. **Conservation of Energy:**
    - A ball of mass \(0.5 \, \text{kg}\) is dropped from a height of \(10 \, \text{m}\). Calculate its speed just before hitting the ground, assuming no air resistance.

---

### 5. Momentum and Collisions

13. **Linear Momentum:**
    - Determine the momentum of a \(3 \, \text{kg}\) object moving with a velocity of \(4 \, \text{m/s}\).

14. **Conservation of Momentum:**
    - Two ice skaters, with masses \(50 \, \text{kg}\) and \(60 \, \text{kg}\), push off each other. If the \(50 \, \text{kg}\) skater moves at \(2 \, \text{m/s}\), find the velocity of the \(60 \, \text{kg}\) skater.

15. **Elastic Collision:**
    - Two balls collide elastically. Ball A has a mass of \(1 \, \text{kg}\) and velocity \(3 \, \text{m/s}\), while Ball B has a mass of \(2 \, \text{kg}\) and is initially at rest. Calculate their velocities after the collision.

16. **Inelastic Collision:**
    - Two cars collide and stick together. Car A has a mass of \(1000 \, \text{kg}\) and is moving at \(15 \, \text{m/s}\), while Car B has a mass of \(1500 \, \text{kg}\) and is initially at rest. Find their common velocity after the collision.

---

### 6. Rotational Mechanics

17. **Torque Calculation:**
    - Calculate the torque produced by a force of \(10 \, \text{N}\) applied at a distance of \(0.4 \, \text{m}\) from the pivot, at an angle of \(90^\circ\).

18. **Moment of Inertia:**
    - Find the moment of inertia of a solid sphere of mass \(5 \, \text{kg}\) and radius \(0.2 \, \text{m}\) about an axis through its center.

19. **Angular Acceleration:**
    - A rotating wheel increases its angular velocity from \(2 \, \text{rad/s}\) to \(6 \, \text{rad/s}\) in \(4 \, \text{s}\). Calculate the angular acceleration.

---

### 7. Oscillatory Motion

20. **Simple Harmonic Motion:**
    - Determine the period of a mass-spring system with a spring constant \(k = 50 \, \text{N/m}\) and a mass of \(2 \, \text{kg}\).

21. **Damped Oscillation:**
    - Explain how the amplitude of an oscillating system decreases over time due to damping.

22. **Forced Oscillations:**
    - For a system with a natural frequency of \(5 \, \text{Hz}\), what happens when it is driven by an external force with a frequency of \(4 \, \text{Hz}\)?

---

### 8. Gravitation

23. **Gravitational Force Calculation:**
    - Calculate the gravitational force between Earth and a satellite of mass \(1000 \, \text{kg}\) in orbit at a distance of \(6.5 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}\) from the Earth's center. (Use \(M_{\text{Earth}} = 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg}\))

24. **Gravitational Potential Energy:**
    - Find the gravitational potential energy of a \(2 \, \text{kg}\) mass at a height of \(10 \, \text{m}\) above the ground. (Use \(g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\))

25. **Kepler’s Laws:**
    - For a planet orbiting the Sun with a semi-major axis of \(1.5 \times 10^{11} \, \text{m}\), calculate its orbital period using Kepler’s Third Law.

26. **Orbital Velocity:**
    - Find the orbital velocity of a satellite in a circular orbit at a height of \(500 \, \text{km}\) above Earth's surface.

---

### 9. Fluid Mechanics

27. **Buoyancy Force:**
    - A metal block of volume \(0.1 \, \text{m}^3\) is submerged in water. Calculate the buoyant force acting on it.

28. **Continuity Equation:**
    - Water flows through a pipe of radius \(0.1 \, \text{m}\) at a speed of \(5 \, \text{m/s}\). The pipe narrows to a radius of \(0.05 \, \text{m}\). Calculate the new velocity of the water.

29. **Bernoulli’s Principle:**
    - A fluid flows through a pipe with a wide section and a narrow section. Explain how the pressure and velocity change between the two sections.

30. **Viscosity:**
    - Derive the expression for the terminal velocity of a small sphere falling through a viscous fluid.

---

### 10. Thermodynamics

31. **First Law of Thermodynamics:**
    - A gas is compressed in a piston, doing \(200 \, \text{J}\) of work while absorbing \(150 \, \text{J}\) of heat. Calculate the change in internal energy.

32. **Heat Transfer Calculation:**
    - How much heat is required to raise the temperature of \(2 \, \text{kg}\) of water from \(20^\circ \text{C}\) to \(80^\circ \text{C}\)? (Use \(c = 4186 \, \text{J/kg} \cdot \text{K}\))

33. **Entropy Change:**
    - Calculate the entropy change when \(1 \, \text{kg}\) of water is heated from \(0^\circ \text{C}\) to \(100^\circ \text{C}\) at constant pressure.

34. **Ideal Gas Law:**
    - A gas occupies a volume of \(3 \, \text{m}^3\) at a pressure of \(2 \, \text{atm}\) and temperature of \(300 \, \text{K}\). How will the volume change if the temperature increases to \(400 \, \text{K}\) at constant pressure?

35. **Carnot Efficiency:**
    - Determine the efficiency of a Carnot engine operating between

 a hot reservoir at \(600 \, \text{K}\) and a cold reservoir at \(300 \, \text{K}\).

---

### 11. Electromagnetism

36. **Coulomb’s Law:**
    - Calculate the electric force between two charges of \(1 \, \text{C}\) and \(2 \, \text{C}\) separated by \(1 \, \text{m}\) in a vacuum. (Use \(k_e = 8.99 \times 10^9 \, \text{N} \cdot \text{m}^2/\text{C}^2\))

37. **Electric Field of a Point Charge:**
    - Find the electric field at a distance of \(0.5 \, \text{m}\) from a charge of \(3 \, \text{C}\).

38. **Magnetic Force on a Moving Charge:**
    - A proton moves with a velocity of \(2 \times 10^6 \, \text{m/s}\) perpendicular to a magnetic field of \(0.1 \, \text{T}\). Calculate the magnetic force acting on the proton. (Use \(q_{\text{proton}} = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}\))

39. **Faraday’s Law of Induction:**
    - A loop of wire with an area of \(0.1 \, \text{m}^2\) is placed in a magnetic field that changes from \(0.5 \, \text{T}\) to zero in \(2 \, \text{s}\). Calculate the induced EMF.

40. **Capacitance Calculation:**
    - Calculate the capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor with an area of \(0.2 \, \text{m}^2\) and separation of \(0.01 \, \text{m}\), filled with air. (Use \(\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{F/m}\))

---

### 12. Quantum Mechanics

41. **Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle:**
    - Calculate the uncertainty in position of an electron moving with a velocity of \(2 \times 10^6 \, \text{m/s}\), knowing the uncertainty in its velocity is \(1 \times 10^3 \, \text{m/s}\). (Use \(m_{\text{electron}} = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \, \text{kg}\))

42. **Energy of a Photon:**
    - Calculate the energy of a photon with a wavelength of \(500 \, \text{nm}\). (Use \(h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J} \cdot \text{s}\))

43. **Photoelectric Effect:**
    - Light with a wavelength of \(400 \, \text{nm}\) shines on a metal surface, causing electrons to be ejected. Calculate the kinetic energy of the ejected electrons if the work function of the metal is \(2 \, \text{eV}\).

44. **Schrödinger Equation:**
    - Solve the time-independent Schrödinger equation for a particle in a one-dimensional infinite potential well.

45. **Wave-Particle Duality:**
    - Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of an electron moving with a velocity of \(1 \times 10^7 \, \text{m/s}\).

---

### 13. Modern Physics

46. **Special Relativity:**
    - A spaceship travels at \(0.8c\) relative to an observer on Earth. Calculate the time dilation experienced by the crew if 5 years pass on Earth.

47. **Mass-Energy Equivalence:**
    - Calculate the energy equivalent of a mass of \(1 \, \text{kg}\). (Use \(c = 3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}\))

48. **Nuclear Decay:**
    - Calculate the activity of a sample of \(^{238}\text{U}\) with a half-life of \(4.5 \times 10^9 \, \text{years}\) and a mass of \(1 \, \text{kg}\).

49. **Blackbody Radiation:**
    - Calculate the wavelength of maximum emission for a blackbody at a temperature of \(5000 \, \text{K}\). (Use Wien’s constant \(b = 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m} \cdot \text{K}\))

50. **Compton Scattering:**
    - A photon of wavelength \(0.01 \, \text{nm}\) scatters off an electron. Calculate the change in wavelength of the photon after scattering at an angle of \(90^\circ\).
